| [`08_ood_clean_paradox.md`](08_ood_clean_paradox.md) | **Why OOD OCMR looks "cleaner" than in-dist val** (2026-06-20, reconciled with `10`/`11`): resolves the red flag that OOD recons looked better than in-distribution. **The blur is mostly the SPLAT renderer (~75%, `docs/10`), common to both — NOT breathing.** val looks worse for two reasons: (1) **resolution** makes that splat blur *visible* on high-res val (inputs 2.4× sharper) and invisible on low-res OCMR — a matched render shows breathing-OFF val is the *sharpest*; (2) **breathing** adds val's *extra* ~25% blur + dark spots (ON→OFF = **−2.31 dB motion PSNR**, primary metric; ON 19.3 below the ~20.6 dB floor, OFF 21.6 above). Black spots minor at full input + refiner-solved (`11`). | [`09_aggft_phase_characterization.md`](09_aggft_phase_characterization.md) | **Aggregator-finetune & per-phase characterization** (2026-06-10), read on motion PSNR (identity floor ≈ 20.6 dB). **Finetuning the aggregator — not just the point head — is what corrects motion** (+2.7–3.2 dB vs +0.6 for head-only, which does ~zero at ED). **All-12-phases is free** (ties the {0,7} specialist). **z/t embeddings worth ~0.8 dB motion**; dropping them bleeds content into out-of-FOV padding (full −3 dB). **~1.7 dB mid-systole dip** (best at ED, worst at peak contraction t4). **Info-contract signal:** `no_zt` (no per-slice input-phase) still reconstructs at ~22.8 dB motion → network infers phase from images (see `04`). | [`10_breathing_failure_mode.md`](10_breathing_failure_mode.md) | **Why the breathing recon is blurry** (2026-06-16, `tools/measure_sharpness.py`). Recon = 0.65× GT sharpness. **Decomposition: ~75% of the blur is the SPLAT renderer, ~25% is the breathing** — even on perfect clean inputs the splat alone drops to 0.74× GT, and the **trained model's sharpness ≈ raw-splat sharpness** (point head fixes *position*, splat caps *detail*). It's **blur, not holes** (coverage_frac 0.713 ≥ 0.69 tissue; only ~5–6% under-covered). Black input pixels are gated out (`intensity>1e-3`). **Motivates the refiner** (biggest, motion-independent lever). Report `_html/08`. |
| [`11_unet_refiner_results.md`](11_unet_refiner_results.md) | **3D UNet refiner results** (2026-06-17/18; impl in `version_history/v2_unet_refiner.md`, motivation `10`). On a **fixed geometry the refiner adds +~1.0 dB motion / +~1.5 dB bbox** (frozen, n=100) and its V_refined motion (20.07) **beats the best no-refiner baseline** (19.28 at a more-trained geometry) → **deblurring the splat beats training the geometry further**. BUT the gain is almost all **dark-spot/coverage correction, NOT deblur** (sharpness 0.668→~0.69× GT, ~6–8% of the gap). A **2D per-slice SSIM term** reaches that ceiling *faster* at no PSNR cost but **plateaus at the same ~0.69× ceiling set by the splat**. Black spots solved; blur needs an *upstream* fix. Reports `_html/10,11,12`; eval `tools/eval_refiner.py`. |

| [`13_limitations_and_improvements.md`](13_limitations_and_improvements.md) | **Limitations decomposition + proven improvements** (2026-06-20): training-free oracle ladder + 4-expert debate on the current best model (joint refiner ep~76), motion-PSNR primary, n=30. **Two independent levers + one transfer risk:** (1) accuracy is **geometry-limited** (~14 dB motion gap: oracle perfect-placement 34.98 vs model 20.62 — the refiner/SSIM work polished the wrong lever); (2) sharpness is **renderer-limited by the 256→518→256 RESIZE, not the kernel** (native-256 splat 0.997× vs current 0.768×; no loss can break it); (3) the true binding constraint is the **domain gap** — single-shot R=8 input drops the model −3.03 dB to near the floor — plus **no real-data metric**. Model does NOT memorize the breathing sim (OOD gain survives +3.96→+4.88). Proven wins: native-res splat (sharpness), multi-draw ensemble (+1.76 dB motion, free), degraded-input aug (necessary). Ranked down: fancy losses, DC loss (already ~34 dB), unfreeze backbone (grad 0.014), k-space. Report: `_html/14_limitations_and_improvements.html`. |
