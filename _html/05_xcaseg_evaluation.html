xcaseg — code analysis & reuse assessment for VGGT-MRI

Repo: github.com/liamjwang/xcaseg · single snapshot commit 151e951 (2025-05-01) · no top-level license · Evaluated 2026-06-10 against the respiratory-motion-simulation direction in docs/01.

Verdict: not usable for your project. DON'T ADOPT
xcaseg is a synthetic X-ray coronary-angiography data generator (a UMich EECS 545 course project), not a cardiac-MRI or motion-simulation tool. Its single motion component — a CardiacWarpTransform — simulates cardiac contraction (which your real CMRxRecon cine already contains, for free, as 12 measured phases), not the respiratory motion you actually need to simulate. The warp is also an uncalibrated, artistic Perlin-noise deformation (no mm, no strain, no literature grounding), and it is welded to a NeRF ray-marching pipeline (nerfstudio) that operates on continuous query positions rather than your discrete monai voxel grid. There is nothing here to drop in. The only takeaway is a design-pattern confirmation you already arrived at independently in docs/01: compose a motion warp before sampling ("deform-then-reslice").

1. What the repo actually is

Despite the suggestive name, "xcaseg" = X-ray Coronary Angiography segmentation, not XCAT. (The internal dataset codename is synthca = synthetic coronary angiography.) It is a heavyweight research/course monorepo that vendors a dozen unrelated packages (nerfstudio, dust3r, mvdust3r, Video-Swin-Transformer, apex, a CroCo fork, …) under pkgs/. The original work — and the only part that matters here — lives in pkgs/xcaseg/xcaseg/datagens/.

Its goal: generate synthetic coronary-angiogram videos with ground-truth vessel masks, to train a 2D vessel-segmentation network without real labeled angiograms. Maturity signals: a single squashed commit, no license file, leftover scratch files (asdfsadf.py, dataset_synthca copy.py), and TODO: disable cardiac warp comments in the hot path. It is a student deliverable, not a maintained library.

2. The generation pipeline (synthca_v3)

One synthetic example is built entirely from a generic CT plus procedural geometry — there is no real angiogram anywhere in the loop:

  1. Load a CT (TotalSegmentatorCTs → volume_from_nifti) as a static density volume — the soft-tissue background.
  2. Grow a synthetic vessel tree (treegen.generate_tree → volume_from_tree): a procedural branching coronary tree voxelized into its own density volume.
  3. Apply a time-dependent cardiac warp (CardiacWarpTransform) to both volumes, composed with a random rigid placement matrix via a SequentialSampleTransform chain.
  4. Differentiable X-ray rendering (voxel_warp_field.py, a nerfstudio Field/Model): march C-arm camera rays (ClinicalCArmView, LAO/RAO + CRA/CAU angles) through the warped volumes, accumulate density, and apply Beer–Lambert transmission exp(−∑density) to get a 2D projection.
  5. Write outputs: per-frame X-ray PNG/npz (combined + tissue-only + tree-only), depth maps, and camera poses — a short beating-heart angiogram clip per object, with the tree channel doubling as the segmentation label.

Everything below the warp — vessel trees, mass attenuation, spectral/material coefficients, C-arm geometry, NeRF renderer — is X-ray-specific and irrelevant to MRI volume reconstruction.

3. The one relevant component: CardiacWarpTransform

datagens/components/cardiac_warp.py — a torch.nn.Module that maps (positions, t) → (warped_positions, t). This is the only motion model in the repo. What it does, mechanically:

  • Periodic time drive. t ∈ [0,1) feeds harmonic signals (sin(2πt)+0.4·sin(4πt) for a global rotation angle; a 3-harmonic sin(2πt)+0.3·sin(4πt+1.5)+0.1·sin(8πt−4.2) for the contraction phase) so the motion loops over a cardiac cycle.
  • Radial contraction. The core displacement is vec_to_center · sq — points move toward/away from the volume center, i.e. a crude myocardial squeeze.
  • Perlin-noise spatial modulation. A fixed random 3-channel 3D fractal-noise field (sampled at each position) multiplies the displacement, so the contraction is spatially non-uniform and randomized per instance.
  • Plus a small global rigid rotation (random axis, angle ≈ 0.05 rad) and a small translation (≈ 0.1 in normalized units) along a random direction.
It is artistic, not anatomical. Magnitudes (0.05 rad, 0.1, contraction_coeff, the ·0.2/·0.6 fudge factors) are hand-tuned to look plausible in a 2D projection. There is no millimeter calibration, no strain model, no literature reference, and no spacing awareness — confirmed by grep: zero occurrences of mm / strain / spacing / diaphragm anywhere. It is randomized domain-randomization motion (maximize variety so a segmenter generalizes), the opposite of the calibrated, reproducible motion your docs/01 requires.

And critically: there is no respiratory motion anywhere in the repo (grep for respir|breath|diaphragm → nothing). Respiratory simulation — the actual gap in your pipeline, since your breath-hold cine lacks it — is simply not addressed here.

The deepest mismatch is conceptual: xcaseg invents cardiac motion because its synthetic angiograms have none. Your data already contains real, measured cardiac motion — the 12 on-disk phase NIfTIs are your supervision target. Injecting xcaseg's random, uncalibrated cardiac warp would actively fight your supervised intensity loss against the real V_gt. And the motion you do need to fabricate — respiratory — isn't in this repo at all.

5. The only thing worth taking (and you already have it)

The reusable insight is a pattern, not code: compose motion as a transform applied to sampling coordinates before they hit the volume, and chain multiple transforms (warp ∘ placement) with a clean SequentialSampleTransform abstraction. That is the same "deform-then-reslice" mechanism you specified in docs/01 §7 (point 4) and that your existing GPU-aug path already implements (re-derive gt_target_volume + re-extract slices after an affine). xcaseg confirms the approach is sound but adds nothing beyond it.

Recommendation. Skip xcaseg entirely. Proceed with path A from docs/01: implement a small, literature-calibrated rigid respiratory transform (SI translation along canonical Z, decoupled per-slice respiratory phase) directly on your cached phases bundle. If you ever want a differentiable, continuous 3D motion field later (e.g. for an Option-B continuous-phase query), the better references are the SVR toolkits already cited in docs/01 §5 — NeSVoR / SVRTK — not this course project.

6. Practical risks if you tried anyway

  • No license → no legal right to reuse the code. Disqualifying on its own for anything beyond reading.
  • nerfstudio coupling → the warp is invoked inside a nerfstudio Field; lifting just CardiacWarpTransform is possible (its forward only needs a Perlin generator + from_axis_angle), but you'd inherit its normalized-coordinate conventions and have to rewrite the sampling for a discrete grid.
  • Course-grade maturity → one squashed commit, scratch files, TODOs in the hot loop, no tests for the warp, no docs. Nothing is guaranteed correct or maintained.
  • Wrong calibration philosophy → it maximizes random variety; you need controlled, physically-meaningful amplitudes. Adapting it would mean replacing its entire motion core anyway.
