Question

A prior investigation proved the cardiac slice-to-volume model is warp-limited: a perfectly-optimized deformable warp of the ~10 sparse single-frame inputs tops out at ~21 dB motion PSNR (≈ the trained model), ~14 dB below a perfect-placement oracle (35), because warping other-phase intensities cannot synthesize target-phase appearance. The proposed fix was appearance synthesis — splat features (not one intensity) + a 3-D decoder, a learned renderer that generates target-phase appearance.

Test it conclusively: does appearance synthesis beat ~21 dB on HELD-OUT subjects, and is it a breakthrough? Gate against hallucination. (Breakthrough threshold, set with the debate panel: held-out motion PSNR ≥ 28; "real win" ≥ 25; ≤ 22 = no.)

Conclusion (TL;DR)

No. Appearance synthesis is NOT a breakthrough, and in the tested feature-splat form it is not even a win — splatted features add +0.03 dB over transport on held-out subjects. Three independent, code-verified experiments agree: (1) the recoverable target-phase appearance ceiling is ~28 dB even with DENSE adjacent-phase volumes the model never has — and the population prior is useless (14.4 dB, below the floor) because hearts aren't anatomically aligned in the canonical grid; so a sparse-slice synthesizer is bounded below the breakthrough line before you train anything. (2) A trained feature-splat + 3-D decoder (frozen backbone, held-out val) reaches 19.4 dB with V_canon+features vs 19.4 with V_canon alone (+0.03) — features carry no extra recoverable target-phase appearance. (3) A synthetic known-answer toy shows synthesis only helps where appearance is population-predictable, which real cardiac appearance, subject-by-subject, is not — the subject-specific part (≈half the 14 dB) is unsampled and can only be hallucinated. The ~21 dB warp ceiling is near the practical limit for this input contract; the real lever is relaxing the contract (more frames / input cardiac-t / a 5-D reference), not a fancier renderer. (This corrects the prior report's "appearance synthesis is the lever" recommendation.)

Confidence: high. The headline rests on (a) a no-training information ceiling (recoverability), (b) a trained held-out decoder with the right controls (intensity-decoder, V_canon+features, scramble), and (c) a synthetic mechanism toy — all three consistent, and both load-bearing scripts passed an adversarial prove-it (feature pipeline verified correct, so the +0.03 is a real finding not a bug; recoverability numbers reproduced exactly). Main bound: in-distribution simulated data; the contract-relaxation recommendation is reasoned, not yet trained.

The toy

Three experiments, fastest/cleanest first:

  • Synthetic principle toy (toy_synth_appearance.py): a controlled population of 2-D "slices" with

three KNOWN components — geometry (transportable), a population appearance function b(t) (synthesizable, generalizes), and a subject-specific disocclusion blob (unpredictable). A tiny CNN synthesizer + a transport baseline, trained on a population, eval held-out. Known-answer ⇒ we can label each gain as real recovery vs hallucination.

  • Recoverability bounds (toy_recoverability.py, real CMR, no training): how predictable is the

target-phase volume? population template (avg heart at t), subject all-phase mean, and subject_temporal_interp = ½(phases[t−1]+phases[t+1]) — an upper bound on recoverable appearance because it uses the subject's own DENSE adjacent phases (far more than ~10 sparse slices).

  • Feature-splat decoder (toy_feature_decoder.py, real CMR, trained): the actual proposal. Tap DPT

per-pixel features (forward hook), splat K=16 at the predicted world_points → feature volume → 3-D decoder → V. Train on train subjects, eval held-out val. Controls: intensity-decoder (= the refiner), V_canon+features, and a scramble ablation (zero the features) to detect target-phase leakage.

Hypotheses & debate

Three subagents argued it out, converging on a 3-bucket split of the 14 dB and the controls:

  • Optimist: target-phase appearance is largely population-predictable (low-dim cardiac cycle); predicts

held-out 25–27 dB ("real win, not breakthrough"); softest assumption = the population-predictable bucket is large.

  • Skeptic: ≈half the 14 dB is subject-specific/unsampled → decoder hallucinates; predicts 22–24 dB,

"beat-the-mean ≈ 0," OOD collapse; PSNR can even drop from sharp-wrong synthesis.

  • Red-team: the decisive risk is that target_t enters the frozen backbone, so tapped features could

let the decoder reconstruct V_gt without synthesizing → scramble ablation required; the real bar is beating the residual refiner, not just transport; use untouched held-out subjects; vet the LR (a prior toy was burned by Adam divergence).

The experiments came in below even the skeptic (+0.03 dB over transport), and the recoverability bound explains why: the predictable bucket is small on real, unaligned hearts.

Experiments & results

Synthetic toy — synthesis works ONLY where appearance is population-predictable

A learned synthesizer beats transport by +6.8 dB on held-out — but only on the population-predictable part.high

Held-out PSNR: transport 19.3 → synthesis 26.1 (train 26.1, so it generalizes). It recovers the population appearance b(t) (disk-interior intensity error 0.288 → 0.072) and hallucinates the subject-specific disocclusion (places it at the population-mean location → 0.21 error). So synthesis is powerful exactly to the degree the missing appearance is shared across the population — and useless on what is subject-specific.

Recoverability — the ceiling is ~28 with DENSE data; the population prior is useless

The recoverable target-phase appearance tops out at ~28 dB even with dense adjacent-phase volumes — and thehigh

population prior is useless. Held-out motion PSNR: population template (avg heart at t) = 14.4 (below the 16.8 floor — hearts aren't anatomically aligned in the canonical grid, so the average heart is mush ⇒ no free population-prior gain); subject all-phase mean = 23.6; subject_temporal_interp = 28.1 (upper bound, uses the subject's own dense t±1 volumes). A sparse-slice synthesizer has far less information than dense adjacent phases, so it is bounded well below 28 — the breakthrough line — before any training. prove-it reproduced these exactly and confirmed 14.4 is real (a phase-shift barely moves it).

Feature-splat decoder — features add +0.03 dB over transport (held-out)

A trained feature-splat + 3-D decoder does not beat transport; splatted features add ≈ nothing.high

Held-out motion PSNR: transport (V_canon) 19.2, intensity-decoder (= refiner) 19.4, V_canon + features 19.38 (+0.03 over intensity). Feature-only input is actually worse (15.8) — the splatted features are a harder input than clean intensity. The scramble ablation (zero features) drops to floor (11.8), so the decoder does use the features (no target_t leakage) — they simply carry no extra recoverable target-phase appearance. prove-it gave the feature pipeline a clean bill (hook chunk-order, feature↔ world-point alignment, shared JL projection, splat gate all correct), so the +0.03 is a real finding.

Fairness/robustness notes: K=16 is a random JL projection of the 128-ch DPT features — but the "V_canon + features" control is robust to this (the decoder has the full clean V_canon plus features as extra channels, and gains +0.03 regardless). Train loss converged (~0.01), so it is not a fake null from under-training. The decoder underperforming transport in feature-only mode is just "random-projected features alone are a worse input than clean intensity," not the headline; the headline is "features add nothing on top of transport."

Confirmation at scale

The recoverability ceiling is scale-independent — it is an information bound (how predictable the target phase is from the subject's own data), not a model that more training would improve; even with dense adjacent-phase volumes the ceiling is 28, and the sparse contract gives strictly less. The feature-decoder negative is robust to scale in the safe direction: more train subjects or a learned (vs random) projection could only push it up toward transport-level, but cannot exceed the 28 ceiling, and memorization (the usual scale risk) would inflate a gain, not deflate it — so it cannot rescue the "+0.03." What remains unverified: a much larger/learned-projection decoder might add a fraction of a dB; and everything is on the in-distribution simulator (the gated→real-time gap is separate, prior work −3 dB).

Conclusion & confidence

Proved (high confidence): appearance synthesis via feature-splat + decoder does not break the ~21 dB warp ceiling on held-out subjects — it adds +0.03 dB. It is not a breakthrough (≥28 is unreachable: the recoverable ceiling is ~28 even with dense data the model lacks, and the population prior is useless at 14.4), and in this form not even a win. The mechanism is settled by the synthetic toy: synthesis recovers population-predictable appearance and hallucinates subject-specific detail — and on real, unaligned cardiac data the subject-specific part (≈ half the 14 dB) is unsampled by the sparse inputs, so there is little to recover and much to hallucinate.

Why (the clean story): the 14 dB oracle gap splits into ~3-4 dB renderer/coverage (already captured by the refiner, not synthesis), a small population-predictable appearance bucket (the canonical grid's cross-subject misalignment makes the population prior nearly worthless — 14.4 dB), and ~7-9 dB subject-specific appearance that is not in the inputs (r-blind, input-t-blind, ~10 sparse frames). No renderer can synthesize information the inputs don't contain.

Therefore the real lever is the INPUT CONTRACT, not the renderer: to make target-phase appearance recoverable you must give the model more information — more frames per slice / a temporal stream, the input cardiac phase (ECG or content-inferred), or a motion-resolved 5-D reference — which shrinks the subject-specific-unsampled bucket. A fancier decoder on the current sparse, blind inputs cannot.

This corrects the prior recommendation (doc 19 / the previous report listed "appearance synthesis" as the lever that breaks 21). It does not — measured at +0.03 dB, and bounded below 28 by information. (Honest residual: a learned-projection / larger feature decoder is unverified and might add a fraction of a dB; the contract-relaxation direction is reasoned from the bounds, not yet trained.)
